Our Origin

Architects
of the Infinite

Founded in 2003 by Dr. Lyra Voss and Caelum Ashford, ASTRVM emerged from a singular belief: that architecture should not merely respond to its environment, but reshape perception of space itself.

We draw inspiration from astrophysical phenomena — the curvature of spacetime, the geometry of black hole event horizons, the fractal nature of cosmic dust — translating these into structures that feel both timeless and otherworldly.

How We Work

The Stellar
Process

01
Phase I — Discovery
Orbital Survey
We begin by mapping the gravitational forces at play — site conditions, client aspirations, cultural context, regulatory constraints, and budgetary physics. No assumption is made. Every parameter is measured, challenged, and understood before a single concept is formed.
2–4 Weeks
02
Phase II — Conception
Nebula Formation
From structured chaos, form emerges. Our designers enter a period of intense generative exploration — sketching, modeling, debating, discarding. We pursue five or six radically different directions before converging on the concept that carries the most cosmic truth.
4–8 Weeks
03
Phase III — Development
Gravitational Pull
The chosen concept is subjected to extreme analytical rigor. Structural engineers, sustainability consultants, and material specialists join the orbital — their inputs pulling and shaping the design until it achieves both formal beauty and technical inevitability.
8–16 Weeks
04
Phase IV — Documentation
Blueprint of Stars
Full construction documentation produced with obsessive precision. Every detail resolved in drawing before it is resolved in matter — because the cost of ambiguity in documentation is always paid on site, in time and money.
12–24 Weeks
05
Phase V — Realization
Stellar Birth
On-site presence throughout construction. Our architects inhabit the project during realization — solving unforeseen conditions with creative intelligence rather than compromise, ensuring the built outcome honors the integrity of the vision through to completion.
Variable
